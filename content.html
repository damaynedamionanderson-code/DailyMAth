The Applied Math Roadmap

My goal is to pursue a degree in Applied Mathematics at UCLA. To prepare for the rigor of a top-tier university, I developed this platform as a high school freshman to bridge the gap between secondary education and university-level analysis. This tool ensures that I am proactively learning and mastering advanced concepts years before I step onto campus.

This curriculum focuses on the core pillars of the UCLA Applied Math major, tailored for early mastery:

Phase I: Algorithmic Foundations

Focuses on Linear Algebra and Discrete Mathematics. These form the basis of modern computation and are essential for my future studies in mathematical modeling.

Phase II: Dynamic Systems

Explores Multivariable Calculus and Ordinary Differential Equations. Understanding how multiple variables change simultaneously is key to solving real-world engineering and physics problems.

Phase III: Computational Mastery

Advanced Vector Analysis and Integral Theory. This level challenges my ability to synthesize complex mathematical proofs, preparing me for the intensity of UCLA's upper-division coursework.

By engaging with this application daily, I am building the technical stamina and analytical discipline required of a future Bruin. Every session is a step toward my goal of contributing to the mathematical sciences at UCLA.
